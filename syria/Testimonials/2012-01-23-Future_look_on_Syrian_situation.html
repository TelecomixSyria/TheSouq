Reach him on Twitter

This article represents my personal opinion, based on readings from Syrian, Arab, and worldwide's political and financial news.

Overview:
The European Union imposed a petrol embargo on Iran, which coincided with the entrance of American flattops in the strait of Hormuz; this will surely lead Iran to close the strait, which will cause a big tension in the area.
On the other hand, it is very likely that within this situation Israel will have a “clear shot” to commit a military act against Iran's reactors...
But of course Iran won't stand idle and will retaliate against American bases in the Gulf, (being the closest), which will open hell-doors in the area as a consequence.
Results of this action will be:
1) Cutting Iran's help to both Syria and Hezbollah
2) Diverting the world views away from Syrian situation, which will give the regime another chance (I will talk about that later)
3) Terminating Iran's threat in the region and ending Russian-Iranian alliance

For what concerns Syrian situation:
The regime will start permitting floating exchange rate, with a wave of prices increase by rates starting from 50% like butane (increase from 250 to 400 SYP) followed by an increase in the combustible and the electricity's prices which will surely lead, eventually, to an unrestrained revolution. And with the lack of safety, crime's percentage will increase.
On the other hand, the fact that the world will be busy with what took place in Iran and the Gulf will give to Syrian regime more chances to obscure events, with more repression and murders.
At this moment citizens will be proposed to choose between hunger and necessity on one side and submission to regime on the other... and of course submission will be the prevalent choice
