Law for the regulation of network Communication against cyber crime

CHAPTER I

Article 1

Definitions

The following terminology used to apply the law is intended by the meanings indicated next to each of them, unless the context indicates otherwise.

Commission: The National organization for network services of the Ministry of communications and modern technology, under the law of electronic signature and network services no.4 of year 2009.

Data: Signals, messages, texts, sounds, moving or still pictures which carry meanings capable of being realized when linked to the specific context.

Computer: A device that uses electronic, electro-magnetic, optical, digital or any other similar kind of technology in order to generate, congregate, store, edit, transmit, access or process data.

Software:A consistent sequence of encoded instructions which can be implemented on a digital device in order to enable needed functions or applications.

Information System: a consistent set of devices, software and their respective arrays.

Network: A connection of computers and informatics systems that allows information exchange or a link between a sender and a receiver or a group of receivers according to specific procedures.

Website: An Informatic system which has a name or an address identifying it, it includes data or services which can be reached through the network, especially the internet.

Network contact: Using network or any other similar informatics system to put information or services which do not have the character of personal correspondence, accessible to general public or a category of it, in a way that every single member can access these information following a specific procedure.

Content: Data or services that can be accessed and exchanged within network contact's framework.
 
Network Service Provider: Any of the service providers that work within network communication's framework including their varieties: Network Access Service Providers, Network Communication Service Providers and Web Hosting Service Providers.
 
Professionalism: The adjective which describes a Network Service Provider when it exercises a professional or profitable activity following a specific business pattern.
 
Network Communication Service Provider: A service provider which allows network communication through one or more websites or similar informatics systems.
 
Web Hosting Service Provider: A service provider which supplies directly or through an intermediary the environment and the data sources needed to store the content in order to develop a website on the network, known as shortcut to the host.
 
Network Access Service Provider: A service provider which allows subscribers to access network, data and services available in it.
 
Malicious Software: Software developed in order to harm computers or informatics systems or websites or networks, to disable their functionalities or to slow them down or to sabotage contents and sources or to collect information about them or their owners or users or their statements without their permission or providing illegal access or usage of these sources.
Spam Mail: Any kind of mail, no matter its content, which is sent through network to others without their desire to receive it.
 
Website Name: A set of alphanumeric symbols dedicated and registered according to specific rules, it identifies a website within a network, especially the internet, and it allows users to reach it.
 
Internet Domain: A group of internet websites names belonging to a singular administrative authority.
 
Top level domain name: The widest domain to which a website belongs to on the internet and it corresponds to the last field of this website's name.
 
National Top Level Domain Name: A standard top level domain name under which are gradated all websites or internet sources directed by a single authority having a national feature.
 
Syrian Top Level Domain Name: The national top level domain name for the Syrian Arab Republic and it is "Syria" or "sy" or any other later adopted domain.
 
Cyber Crime: A crime committed by using computers or network or located in them.
 
Digital Evidence: Digital data stored on a computer or on informatics systems or transmitted through them which can be used to confirm or deny a cyber crime.
 
Traffic Data: Any data being exchanged within network's communication framework, in particular determining source and destination of the connection, websites being accessed and connection time and duration.
 
Electronic Investigations: Legally authorized access to information or to data traffic exchanged on informatic systems or on networks in order to investigate, track and control these processes.
 
Privacy: Individual's rights to protect his personal, personal related and familiar secrets, his correspondences, his reputation, the inviolability of his house and of his personal property and his rights to prevent their permeation and exposure.
 
Organized Gang: A group of people or activities usually featured by a central organization aiming to exercise criminal activities either at a regional, or national, or international level.
 
Credit Card: A standard dimensioned card usually issued by banks or financial institutions used for payment transactions, withdrawing credit or funds or transferring them through a bank account or portfolio.

CHAPTER II

Article 2

General Responsibilities

 
(A) It is up to network service providers to save a copy of the content they have stored in case they have it and to preserve data traffic, this allows verification of the identity of those people who contribute in developing content within network and will be for a period determined by the commission. Content and data traffic will be subjected to professional secrecy and the commission will set the system and the technical standards needed to apply the provisions of this paragraph after coordination with the National Media Council regarding the means of network communication according to the media law in force.
 
(B) It is up to network service providers to furnish information requested by the competent judicial authorities.

Article 3

Responsibilities of the Network Access Service Provider

(A) Network access service provider is allowed to temporarily store data exchanged through it and this may be done directly or by entities in charge of doing so on condition to improve service offered or else this would lead to a violation of the users' privacy or of their personal data. Stored data should not be changed in any way and it is necessary to take all needed measures in order to deny any illegal access to the stored content and to erase it as soon as it is no more needed.
The commission will set the system and the technical standards needed to apply the provisions of this paragraph.
 
(B) Network access service provider is not responsible of controlling data stored in it or exchanged through it but within the conditions drawn up in paragraph A of this article, however it's up to the provider to prevent access to any part of the content within 24 hours in the event of receiving the order from the competent judicial authority.
 
(C) Network access service provider is in charge of offering the technical means which allow subscribers to prevent their access to certain websites or to some varieties of content within network when requested by them.
The commission will set the system and the technical standards needed to apply the provisions of this paragraph. 

Article 4

Web Hosting Service Provider responsibilities

(A) Web hosting service provider must not allow any change of the content stored in it but the one made by the same content's owner or administrator or by request or permission of one of them.
 
(B) Web hosting service provider is not responsible of controlling data stored in it and it must withdraw any part of the content from circulation or prevent access to it within 24 hours in case any of the following events occurred:
  1. Receiving the request from the owner or the direct administrator of the content
  2. Receiving the order from the competent judicial authority
  3. Recognizing the actual nature of the content as illegal according to the provisions of this law and its operational instructions
 
(C) Network access and network communication service providers are allowed to exercise at the same time the role of web hosting service providers.

Article 5

Identifying a website to the Network Communication Service Provider

(A) It is up to any professional network communication service provider to highlight on its website the following statements:
  1. Name of the website's owner or services offered to connect through the network, his address and commercial register, if present
  2. Name of the website's administrator in charge, his address and contact's means and details
  3. Any statement required by the law, especially by the media law in force
  4. Any other statement required by the law's executive instructions
 
(B) Non-professional Network communication service providers are not required to declare the statements mentioned in paragraph A of this article but they must identify the web hosting service provider to which they are related as well as the name of the website's owner  or services offered to connect through the network, his address and contact's means and details.
These present statements are subjected to professional secrecy but they must be provided to the competent judicial authority when ordered to do so

Article 6

Reporting the illegal nature of the network's content

(A) It is the right of any aggrieved party to denounce the illegal nature of any content published by the service provider at issue to the network communication or to the web hosting service providers, along with its right to request its removal, revision or correction.
Executive instructions for this law will define the statements that must be included within the news and the mechanisms that must be followed in order to validate them.
 
(B) Actual knowledge drawn up in paragraph B of Article 4 of this law is considered the resultant action in case the news agree with the provisions mentioned in paragraph A of this article and that will depend on the determination made by the executive instructions for the law.
 
(C) In all cases the provisions mentioned in this article do not prohibit the aggrieved party from appealing to the court according to the general rules and do not deny the network communication or the web host service providers their right to prove the invalidity of the news' contents and to demand compensation.

Article 7

Website blocking

A website or a similar facility used by the network communication service provider cannot be obscured completely or partially, temporarily or permanently unless one of the following events occurred:
  1. Order from the competent judicial authority
  2. In case the network communication service provider disclaimed his commitments by publishing its statements not according to the provisions of article 5 of this law and that will be till the violation will be removed.

Article 8

Violation of the obligation of saving a copy of the content and of traffic data

(A) The network service provider which neglects its commitment to save a copy of stored contents and of traffic data which allow to identify people contributing to the development of the content on the network will be punished with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP.
 
(B) In case the neglection indicated in paragraph (A) of this article is intentional and leads to the impediment of a crime investigation or of its initiation or of crime prevention then punishment shall be imprisonment from 3 months up to 2 years and a fine from 200'000 up to 1'000'000 SYP.

Article 9

Disclosure of data and information

A network service provider which discloses content, data or information stored in it by violating professional secrecy obligations shall be punished with a fine from 200'000 up to 1'000'000 SYP while preserving the aggrieved party's right to claim compensation.

Article 10

Changing content

A network service provider that illegally changes content, data or information stored shall be punished by imprisonment from 6 months up to 3 years and a fine from 200'000 up to 1'000'000 SYP while preserving the aggrieved party's right to claim compensation.
 
Article 11
Abstention from answering the competent judicial authority's injunction
A network service provider that abstains from answering an injunction made by the competent judicial authority to withdraw any content stored or part of it from circulation or to prevent access to it shall be sentenced according to the punishment prescribed when offending the authority by not implementing its decision.
 
Article 12

Abstention from deleting, editing or correcting illegal content

(A) A network service provider that after having actual evidence abstains from deleting, editing or correcting illegal content shall be punished according to the provisions of this law by a fine from 200'000 up to 1'000'000 SYP.
 
(B) In case the abstention indicated in paragraph A of this article leads to the impediment of a crime's investigation or of its initiation or of crime prevention then punishment shall be imprisonment from 2 months up to 3 years and a fine from 200'000 up to 1'000'000 SYP.
 
(C) Anyone who spreads the news about illegal content shall be sentenced according to the punishment prescribed in paragraph A of this article in case of actual evidence of the falsity of this news, as drawn up in article 6 of this law.
 
Article 13
Application of the Trade Act
Providing professional network services is considered a business within the meaning of the Trade Act in force
 
Article 14
Preservation of the author and the property's rights
 
Any content published on the network is subjected to the laws preserving the author, to the related laws, to the preservation of ownership rights as well as the intellectual, commercial, industrial rights and copyright.
 

CHAPTER III
Fighting Cyber-Crime
 

Article 15

Illegal access to an informatic system

(A) Anyone who intentionally accesses a computer or an informatic system or a website on the internet illegally without having permission or the right or the authority to do so shall be punished with a fine from 20'000 up to 100'000 SYP.
 
(B) In case the subject makes a copy of the data or the information or the designs he managed to reach or he deletes, distorts, changes, uses, imitates, discloses them he shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 months up to 2 years.
 
Article 16
Domain or website's name occupation
Anyone who occupies a website's name without its owner acknowledgment or limits the domain owner's privileges on the internet or controls this domain, shall be punished by imprisonment from 1 up to 6 months and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP.
 
Article 17
Prevention of accessing the service
Anyone who intentionally hinders or prevents access by any means to an informatic system or to network or blocks or defects it or prevents it from working properly or denies by any means access to services or programs or websites or data sources or information about them shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 months up to 2 years and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP.
 
 
Article 18
Data Interception
(A) Anyone who intentionally and illegally intercepts or picks up information exchanged through an informatic system or network or taps on them shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 months up to 2 years and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP.
 
(B) Anyone who uses fraud to achieve personal or secret data from users on an informatic system or network and allows usage of these data for criminal purposes shall be punished by imprisonment from 1 up to 6 months and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP.
 
Article 19
Creating and using malware software
(A) Anyone who develops malware software and promotes them to be used for criminal purposes shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 up to 5 years and with a fine of 500'000 up to 2'500'000 SYP.
 
(B) Anyone who uses by any means malware software of any kind in order to damage computers or informatic systems or network shall be punished by imprisonment from 6 months up to 3 years and with a fine from 200'000 up to 1'000'000 SYP.
 
Article 20
Sending spam mail
Anyone who sends spam mail to others within the case that the recipient cannot stop them from arriving or stopping them incurs additional expenses on the recipient shall be punished with a fine from 20'000 up to 100'000 SYP.
 
Article 21
Fraud
(A) Anyone used a computer or network to take over transferred money, or real estate, or information, or valuable software or vouchers that include a commitment, a discharge or any other kind of financial privileges that include a commitment, or a discharge; using fraud to a victim, or deceiving any informatic system owned or controlled by the victim by any means, shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 up to 5 years and with a fine from 500'000 up to 2'500'000 SYP.
 
(B) Punishment shall be temporary imprisonment and a fine from 500'000 up to 2'500'000 SYP in the following cases:
  1. If the crime occurred on 3 people or more
  2. If the amount extorted exceeds 1'000'000 SYP
  3. If the fraud is against a bank or a financial institution
(C) Discretionary extenuating reasons are not applied unless the injured drops his personal right.
 
Article 22
Illegal use of credit cards
(A) Anyone who obtains a credit card credentials by the use of computers or network without any right to do so shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 months up to 2 years and with a fine from 500'000 SYP up to 2'500'000
 
(B) Anyone who:
  1. Frauds a credit card
  2. Uses a forged, robbed or missing credit card for paying or withdrawing cash
Shall be punished by imprisonment from 3 up to 5 years and a fine from 500'000 up to 2'500'000 SYP
 
 

Article 23

Violation of private life

Anyone publishes information which violate any person's privacy without his consent shall be punished by imprisonment from 1 to 6 months and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP, even if this information is correct.
 

Chapter IV

The Judicial Police and Digital Evidence
 

Article 24

Competent judicial police

 
(A) A Competent judicial police takes form within the Interior Ministry, investigates cyber crimes, collects digital evidence needed, arrests the crime's actors and takes them to the competent court in order to be punished.
 
(B) The competent judicial police mentioned in paragraph (A) of this article has recourse to permanent or temporary experts from the Ministry of Defense, the Ministry of Justice and the Ministry of Communications and Technology in order to fulfill their entrusted tasks, and those experts swear for judicial truth.
 

Article 25

Digital evidence

(A) It is up to the court to assess the value of digital evidence, on condition to verify the following:
  1. Computers and informatic systems from which digital evidence comes from should work properly
  2. No changes applied to the digital evidence presented to the court for its period of storage
(B) Digital evidence provided to the court in addition to the 2 conditions indicated in paragraph (A) of this article is considered legitimate unless the contrary is proved
 

Article 26

Digital tracking, inspection and seizure.

(A) Judicial police officers are allowed to carry out digital investigations by the competent judicial authority's authorization
 
(B) Software are considered as a material matter which may be inspected and adjusted according to the rules provided within the Code of Criminal procedure
 
(C) Computers and software which are connected to the suspect's devices may be inspected whatever is their position within location limits assigned to the suspect
 
(D) Network service providers must commit to maintain the confidentiality of the proceedings conducted by the competent judicial police in all cases
 
(E) Informatic systems owners or administrators that took part to a cyber crime must set their systems in order to allow the judicial police officers to inspect and control data, information and software, to obtain a copy of them and when necessary to make them control computers or software used or part of their components.
 
(F) Anyone who violates the provisions of this article shall be punished by imprisonment from 1 to 6 months and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP
 

Chapter V

General and Final Provisions
 

Article 27

Applying the law on network service providers

The provisions mentioned in Chapter II of this Act are applied to any network service provider having within the Syrian Arab Republic an actual management center or a fixed branch from which it presently exercises its business activity, regardless of its nationality and of the place where it was first set up and where headquarters are located in case it is a legal person and regardless of the place where the technical arrays it uses are located.
 

Article 28

Applying criminal laws

 
(A) If the Syrian penal codes is applicable on one of the crimes mentioned in this law then the maximum punishment shall be applied
 
(B) The minimum limit of the penalty prescribed shall be doubled for any of the crimes mentioned within the other Syrian penal codes in the following:
  1. If the crime is committed using the network or happened within it;
  2. If the crime occurred on a computer or an informatic system in order to influence its functionality or its data or the digital evidence stored in it
 

Article 29

Public releases and incitement to commit crimes

(A) Anyone who incites or promotes to commit any of the crimes mentioned within the penal law in force by using network shall be punished by imprisonment from 6 months up to 3 years and with a fine from 100'000 up to 500'000 SYP.
 
(B) In case action mentioned in paragraph (A) of this article is committed by using the internet, punishment will not be less than a year of imprisonment or less than 250'000 SYP fine.
 
 
 

Article 30 

 

Stiffening penalty

Stiffening penalty is done according to the general rules mentioned within the penal code for tightening punishment in the following cases:
  1. If the subject of the crime affects the State or public safety
  2. If the crime is committed by an organized gang
  3. If the crime's victim is a minor or equivalents
  4. If the offender takes advantage of his career to commit the crime
 
 

Article 31

Inception of the crime

 

 
Anyone who attempts to commit a misdemeanor provided for in this law will be punished in accordance with the provisions in the Penal Code
Quiconque tente de commettre un délit prévu dans cette loi sera sanctionné en accord avec les déclarations du Code Pénal
 

Article 32

Information networks are public

The Internet is counted as part of the public implements drawn up within the Penal Code and the penal laws
 

Article 33

Judicial specialization

(A) Crimes drawn up in this law will be judged according to the regional, self, personal and comprehensive powers drawn up within the Penal Code
 
(B) The Syrian Top-level Domain name is considered, and treated as Syrian soil, regarding to this law.
 
 

Article 34

Expropriation

(A) Without prejudice against the rights of others in good will, the court judges the expropriation of computers, softwares or of any device used in order to commit any of the crimes mentioned in this law
 
(B) It is also possible to sentence the cessation or the closure of the website or of any similar system used to commit any of the crimes drawn up in this law in case the crime has been committed by the awareness of the owner of this website or system
 

Article 35

Criminal procedure

The effective criminal procedure law is applied to anything not provided within the Terms of Procedure mentioned in this law
 
 

Article 36

Reconciliating situations

It is up to the Internet service providers to reconcile their situations with the terms in chapter 2 of this law within 6 months from the date of its issuing.
