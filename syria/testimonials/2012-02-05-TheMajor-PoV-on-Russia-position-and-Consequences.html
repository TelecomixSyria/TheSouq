This is my own opinion, or in fact an idea just hit me, based on the Russian history.


Russia may advice Assad to apply the "multi-party" law, and then resigns or step down. According to the Syrian constitution, in case of president resignation, the country will be ruled by the chief of parliament (not vise president), which will force opposition to come for a dialogue, and (of course a new elections). Assad will be the head of "his own party" and the law will allow him to do that, thus he can elect himself again. In worst cases (for Assad) he will be elected again after 4 years.
